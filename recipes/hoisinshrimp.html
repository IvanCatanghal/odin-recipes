Sizzling Hoisin Shrimp

If you're like us, you've seen a lot of stir-fries in your day. But thanks to our hoisin shrimp marinade, aromatic rice, and a sprinkle of nutty sesame seeds, this one is bound to top them all. It cooks super quickly and brings a tour de force of tangy, sweet, and savory flavors that even the pickiest of eaters will love. Sounds like a win-win to us.

Ingredients

  • 2 unit - Ginger
  • 1 clove - Garlic
  • 3/4 cup - Jasmine Rice
  • 2 tablespoon - Hoisin Sauce
  • 1 tablespoon - Sesame Seeds
  • 1 thumb - Ginger
  • 1 unit - Lime
  • 10 ounce - Shrimp
  • 6 ounce - Green beans
  • 2 teaspoon - Vegetable oil
  • 1 tablespoon - Butter
  • Salt
  • Pepper

Instructions

  1. • Adjust rack to top position and preheat oven to 425 degrees. Wash and dry produce. • Trim and thinly slice scallions, separating whites from greens. Peel and mince or grate ginger. Peel and mince garlic. Quarter lime.
  2. • Melt 1 TBSP butter in a small pot over medium-high heat. Add scallion whites and half the ginger; cook until fragrant, 1 minute.
  3. • Add rice, ¾ cup water (1½ cups for 4 servings), and a big pinch of salt to pot with aromatics. Bring to a boil, then cover and reduce to a low simmer. Cook until rice is tender, 15-18 minutes. • Keep covered off heat until ready to serve.
  4. • While rice cooks, rinse shrimp* in cold water and pat dry with paper towels. Toss in a medium bowl with garlic, half the hoisin (all the hoisin for 4 servings), and remaining ginger. Season with salt and pepper. • Set shrimp aside to marinate for at least 5 minutes.
  5. • Meanwhile, trim green beans if necessary; toss on a baking sheet with a drizzle of oil and a pinch of salt and pepper. • Roast on top rack, tossing halfway through, until browned and slightly crisp, 12-15 minutes.
  6. • Heat a drizzle of oil in a large pan over medium-high heat. Add marinated shrimp and cook, stirring, until shrimp are opaque and cooked through, 3-4 minutes. • Divide rice between plates; top with green beans and shrimp. Sprinkle with scallion greens and as many sesame seeds as you like. Serve with lime wedges on the side.
